Statistics & Data Science at BYU

Hey, I'm James.

I'm a Statistics student at BYU with a Data Science emphasis and minors in CS and Information Systems. For me, data isn’t just an academic exercise—it’s a practical lever for solving real-world problems. If a task is repetitive, I’ll automate it; if a system is fragile, I want to build a version that holds up; if a decision needs to be made, I want to look at the numbers.

Right now, I split my time between working as a research assistant, TA-ing for an introductory Information Systems course, and writing custom automation scripts and little web apps to make life a bit easier.

When I'm not in an IDE or a spreadsheet, I’m usually hanging out with my wife, Megan, and our two young daughters here in Utah. Otherwise, you can find me playing Magic: The Gathering or Minecraft, or trying to memorize the script to Monty Python and the Holy Grail.

This site is a home for my résumé, some of my favorite projects, and the best ways to get in touch.
