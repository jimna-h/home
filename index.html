James Bruce

BYU Data Science Student.

Hi, I’m James. I’m from Boise, Idaho, which means I have a deep respect for mountains, seasonal weather, and the idea that “outside” is a real place you can visit when you are not in the middle of something important, like reorganizing a spreadsheet or shuffling up a deck. I play Magic: The Gathering, and I love it in the specific way that makes you say, “This is a fun hobby,” while secretly building color-coded plans for how to achieve maximum nonsense with maximum style. I also genuinely love spreadsheets. Not in a “look at me being productive” way, but in a “this grid is a magical realm and I am its chosen wizard” way. If you have ever felt joy from a perfectly behaving formula, or rage from one tiny typo ruining everything, then congratulations, we are basically family.

Music-wise, I mostly listen to fun., The Beatles, and Twenty One Pilots, which means I can go from upbeat to existential in about twelve seconds and feel normal about it. Movie-wise, my favorite is Monty Python and the Holy Grail, and yes, I quote it more than is socially necessary. I served my mission in Mesa, Arizona, and in Scotland and Ireland, which gave me the full experience of desert heat and then the kind of rain that feels personal. If you want a summary of my personality, it is probably “enthusiastic nerd who can thrive in both sunshine and sideways wind, and will still find a way to make a joke about it.”

These days, my favorite chapter is at home. I’ve been married to Megan for three years, and she is my best friend and the actual hero of our story. Our daughter Jemma is about to turn two, which means our house runs on curiosity, snacks, and sudden bursts of energy that cannot be explained by science. We are also expecting our daughter Mabel in three months, so we are currently in the “excited, grateful, and why are there so many tiny socks?” phase. If you are into Magic, spreadsheets, and a family life that is equal parts love and mild chaos, welcome to my corner of the internet.
